# Phase 1: CLARIFY - Understand Intent

## Overview

**Goal:** Understand WHAT the user wants and WHY before deciding HOW to do it.

**When to Use:** At the start of any new task, when requirements are vague, or when you feel uncertain about goals.

**Success Criteria:** You can accurately summarize the user's intent back to them.

## The Process

### Step 1: Explore Context

Before asking questions, understand the situation:

**Check for:**
- Previous conversation context
- Existing documents or files
- Related work already done
- Constraints (time, resources, standards)

**Actions:**
- Review any provided materials
- Check file system if relevant
- Note any obvious constraints

### Step 2: Ask Clarifying Questions (ONE AT A TIME)

**Critical Rule:** Ask ONE question per message. If a topic needs more exploration, break it into multiple questions.

**Why one at a time?**
- Users can answer quickly
- Answers inform follow-up questions
- Prevents overwhelming the user
- Creates natural dialogue

**Key Question Categories:**

#### Purpose Questions
- "Why are we doing this?"
- "What problem does this solve?"
- "What would success look like?"

#### Audience Questions
- "Who is this for?"
- "What do they care about?"
- "What do they already know?"

#### Scope Questions
- "What's definitely in scope?"
- "What's definitely out of scope?"
- "Are there any constraints I should know about?"

#### Success Criteria Questions
- "How will we know this is done well?"
- "What would make you satisfied with the result?"
- "Are there any specific requirements or standards?"

#### Context Questions
- "Has anything been tried before?"
- "What led to this request?"
- "Is there a deadline or timeline?"

### Step 3: Confirm Understanding

Before proceeding to DESIGN, summarize what you heard:

**Format:**
```
"Let me make sure I understand:

**Goal:** [One sentence summary]

**Success looks like:** [Criteria]

**Constraints:** [Key limitations]

**Audience:** [Who it's for]

Is that correct? Anything I missed?"
```

**Wait for confirmation before proceeding.**

## Question Patterns

### Multiple Choice (Preferred When Possible)

When you can anticipate likely answers, offer choices:

```
"For the tone of this document, which direction feels right?

A) Formal and authoritative
B) Conversational and friendly
C) Direct and concise
D) Something else?"
```

### Open-Ended (When Exploring)

When you need to understand something new:

```
"What would make this offsite successful in your mind?"
```

### Sequential (Breaking Down Complex Topics)

For complex requirements, explore one aspect at a time:

```
Message 1: "What's the primary goal - to inform, persuade, or document?"
Message 2: [After answer] "Who is the main audience?"
Message 3: [After answer] "Are there any constraints on length or format?"
```

## Red Flags - STOP and Clarify

| Thought | Reality | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| "This seems straightforward" | Simple tasks hide assumptions | Ask at least 2-3 clarification questions |
| "I know what they want" | You might know what you would want | Confirm, don't assume |
| "Let me start on what I understand" | Partial understanding → partial solution | Complete CLARIFY first |
| "They'll correct me if I'm wrong" | Corrections cost time and trust | Get it right first |
| "I don't want to bother them with questions" | Good questions show competence | Ask necessary questions |

## Anti-Patterns

### ❌ The Question Dump

```
"I need to know:
1. What's the goal?
2. Who's the audience?
3. What's the timeline?
4. What format?
5. Any constraints?
6. What's the budget?
7. Who else is involved?
8. What has been tried before?"
```

**Problem:** Overwhelming, user won't answer all thoughtfully

### ✅ The Dialogue Approach

```
"First, what's the primary goal of this project?"
[Wait for answer]

"Got it. And who is the main audience?"
[Wait for answer]

"Thanks. What's your timeline?"
```

## Examples

### Example 1: Writing Task

**User:** "Help me write an email to the client."

**❌ Poor Response:**
"Sure, what's it about?"

**✅ Good Response:**
"I'd be happy to help. To make sure the email hits the right tone and achieves your goal, let me ask a few questions.

First, what's the primary purpose of this email - to inform, request something, apologize, or something else?"

[After answer]
"Understood. And who specifically is the recipient - someone you have a close relationship with, or more formal?"

[Continue one question at a time...]

### Example 2: Analysis Task

**User:** "Sales are down, help me figure out why."

**❌ Poor Response:**
"Let me analyze your sales data."

**✅ Good Response:**
"I'll help you analyze this systematically. First, let me understand the situation better.

When did you first notice the sales drop - was it sudden or gradual?"

[After answer]
"Thanks. What's your hypothesis - do you have any initial thoughts on what might be causing it?"

[Continue one question at a time...]

### Example 3: Planning Task

**User:** "I need to plan a team offsite."

**❌ Poor Response:**
"Great! How about a hiking retreat? Or maybe a cooking class? Or we could do an escape room..."

**✅ Good Response:**
"I'd love to help plan this. Let me start by understanding what you're hoping to achieve.

Is the primary goal team building, strategic planning, celebrating a milestone, or something else?"

[After answer]
"Got it. How many people will be attending?"

[Continue one question at a time...]

## Transition to DESIGN

**When to Move On:**
- You can accurately summarize the goal
- You understand success criteria
- You know constraints and audience
- User has confirmed your understanding

**How to Transition:**
```
"Perfect, I have a clear understanding now. 

**Summary:** [Brief recap]

Next, I'll develop 2-3 different approaches for how we could tackle this. I'll present the options with trade-offs and my recommendation. Sound good?"
```

## Quick Checklist

- [ ] Explored existing context
- [ ] Asked questions ONE AT A TIME
- [ ] Covered purpose, audience, scope, success criteria
- [ ] Summarized understanding back to user
- [ ] Got explicit confirmation
- [ ] Ready to move to DESIGN phase

## Remember

**Clarification is not overhead - it's the foundation of good work.**

5 minutes of good clarification saves 30 minutes of rework.

**The Rule:** If you haven't confirmed understanding, you're not done with CLARIFY.
