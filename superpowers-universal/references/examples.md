# Superpowers Universal - Complete Examples

## Example 1: Writing an Important Email

### Scenario
Need to write an email to a client explaining a project delay.

---

## CLARIFY Phase

**AI:** "Before I draft this email, let me understand the situation better. What's the primary goal of this email - to inform, apologize, or request an extension?"

**User:** "Both inform and request a one-week extension."

**AI:** "Who is the recipient - a technical contact or business stakeholder?"

**User:** "The project manager, business side."

**AI:** "What's the reason for the delay that we should communicate?"

**User:** "We discovered an integration issue that requires redesign."

**AI:** "Understood. So the goal is to inform the PM of an integration issue causing delay, request a one-week extension, while maintaining trust. Is that correct?"

**User:** "Yes."

**→ CLARIFY Complete ✓**

---

## DESIGN Phase

**AI:** "Here are two approaches:

**Option A: Direct and Brief**
- State delay upfront
- Explain technical reason briefly
- Request extension
- **Pros:** Respects their time
- **Cons:** May seem abrupt

**Option B: Relationship-Focused** ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- Acknowledge partnership first
- Frame as quality investment
- Context before news
- **Pros:** Maintains trust, shows care
- **Cons:** Longer read

**My Recommendation: Option B**
Given that this is an important client relationship and the delay is significant, maintaining trust is as important as the timeline.

**Design Details:**

**Structure:**
1. Opening: Acknowledge partnership
2. The News: Clear delay statement
3. Context: Brief explanation of integration issue
4. Our Response: What we're doing
5. Ask: Extension request
6. Closing: Quality commitment

**Flow:** Warm → Factual → Action-oriented

Does this approach feel right?"

**User:** "Yes, but keep the technical explanation very brief."

**AI:** "Noted. I'll keep technical details to one sentence."

**→ DESIGN Approved ✓**

---

## PLAN Phase

**AI:** "Here's the plan:

### Task 1: Draft opening and news sections
**Action:** Write partnership acknowledgment and delay statement
**Verify:** Opening is warm but direct, timeline is clear

### Task 2: Draft context section
**Action:** Explain integration issue in one sentence
**Verify:** Technical detail is minimal

### Task 3: Draft response and closing sections
**Action:** Describe mitigation actions and request extension
**Verify:** Shows proactive response, ask is clear

### Task 4: Full review and polish
**Action:** Read entire email, check tone and clarity
**Verify:** Ready to send

Ready to proceed?"

**User:** "Yes."

**→ PLAN Approved ✓**

---

## EXECUTE Phase

**AI:** "**Task 1 Complete ✓**

Draft:

Subject: Project Timeline Update - [Project Name]

Hi [Name],

I wanted to reach out personally about [Project Name]. We've hit an unexpected integration challenge that will require us to extend the timeline by one week, with a new target completion of [Date].
---

Moving to Task 2..."

[Continues through all tasks...]

**AI:** "**All Tasks Complete ✓**

Full email drafted."

## VERIFY Phase

- [x] Requests extension

## Example 3: Analyzing a Business Problem

### Scenario
Sales have dropped 20% this quarter. Need to analyze why.

---

## CLARIFY Phase

**AI:** "Before we analyze, let's clarify. Is the goal to identify causes, find solutions, or both?"

**User:** "Identify top 3 causes first."

**AI:** "What data do we have access to?"

**User:** "Sales CRM, some customer emails, industry reports."

**AI:** "When did you first notice the drop?"

**User:** "Started in month 2 of the quarter."

**AI:** "Any hypotheses already?"

**User:** "Maybe competitor launched something, or our pricing is off."

**→ CLARIFY Complete ✓**

---

## DESIGN Phase

**AI:** "Three analysis approaches:

**Option A: Data-First**
- Pro: Thorough, objective
- Con: Time-intensive (2-3 days)

**Option B: Hypothesis-Driven**
- Pro: Fast if hypotheses are right (4-6 hours)
- Con: May miss other causes

**Option C: Mixed Approach** ⭐ RECOMMENDED
- Pro: Balanced speed and thoroughness
- Con: Moderate time investment

**My Recommendation: Option C**
Quick data scan + test your hypotheses + deep dive if needed.

**Timeline:**
1. Data extraction and scan (1 hour)
2. Hypothesis A testing (2 hours)
3. Hypothesis B testing (2 hours)
4. Alternative exploration (if needed)
5. Root cause ranking (1 hour)

Does this approach work?"

**User:** "Yes, go with Option C."

**→ DESIGN Approved ✓**

---

## PLAN Phase

**AI:** "Analysis plan:

### Task 1: Data extraction
**Action:** Export Q2 sales data from CRM
**Verify:** Data complete and clean

### Task 2: Pattern identification

**Action:** Research competitor launches
**Verify:** Evidence for/against

### Task 5: Alternative cause exploration
**Action:** Check seasonality, team changes, etc.
**Verify:** All plausible causes considered

### Task 6: Root cause ranking
**Action:** Rank causes by impact and evidence

Ready to start?"

**→ PLAN Approved ✓**

## Key Patterns Across Examples

1. **CLARIFY always asks questions first** - Never assume
2. **DESIGN always offers alternatives** - Show trade-offs
3. **PLAN always breaks into small tasks** - Make manageable
4. **EXECUTE always shows progress** - Keep user informed
5. **VERIFY always checks against original intent** - Ensure alignment

These patterns apply to any domain: writing, planning, analysis, design, problem-solving.
